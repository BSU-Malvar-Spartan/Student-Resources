## How to Find Online Resources

Finding quality online resources is an essential skill for students. Here are some strategies:

1. **Use reputable learning platforms** – Websites like Coursera, Khan Academy, and freeCodeCamp offer structured, peer-reviewed content.
2. **Check official documentation** – Languages and frameworks (e.g., Python, React) maintain official docs that are always up to date.
3. **Search with specific terms** – Adding "tutorial", "guide", or "documentation" to your search terms helps filter noise.
4. **Verify credibility** – Prefer resources from universities (.edu), well-known organizations, or widely endorsed community sites.
5. **Look for community recommendations** – Platforms like Reddit (r/learnprogramming), Stack Overflow, and Discord servers often point to high-quality resources.

4. Add it under the appropriate category in this file (or create a new category).
5. Open a pull request with a short description of the resource you are adding.

## Tips for Evaluating an Online Resource

- ✅ Content is regularly updated or recently reviewed.
- ✅ Clear learning objectives are stated.
- ✅ Examples and exercises are included.
- ✅ Community reviews or ratings are positive.
- ✅ Author/organization credentials are verifiable.
- ⚠️ Be cautious of resources with no author, no date, or that ask for payment upfront without a free preview.
